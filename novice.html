TASK 1

Creating Images & Spotting Bias

‚è±Ô∏è 20-25 minutes

Objective: Use AI to generate images and think critically about bias.

Tool: Microsoft Copilot (Standard version)

Instructions:

  1. Open Microsoft Copilot (no agents selected).
  2. Type a prompt relevant to your field:
    Create a photorealistic image of a lawyer in a courtroom.

    Create a photorealistic image of a police officer.

    Create a photorealistic image of a business meeting.
  3. Examine the four images. What do you notice about:
    • Gender representation?
    • Ethnicity representation?
    • Power dynamics?
    • UK vs. international context?
  4. Now try a more specific prompt:
    Create a photorealistic image of a diverse group of three UK police officers (including women and people of different ethnicities) speaking calmly with members of the public in a British town centre.
  5. Compare the results.

ü§î Reflection

The first prompt likely showed bias (e.g., white males in authority) because AI is trained on internet data containing stereotypes. For professional fields like Law and Policing, these stereotypes can be harmful.

Key Learning: Be specific and critical. AI will not automatically generate inclusive images.

‚úì Success Criteria

  • Generated at least two sets of images
  • Can identify differences between vague and specific prompts
  • Understand that AI-generated images may contain biases
TASK 2

Summarising Texts with NotebookLM

‚è±Ô∏è 25-30 minutes

Objective: Use "source-grounded" AI to safely summarise documents.

Tool: NotebookLM (free Google tool)

Instructions:

  1. Go to notebooklm.google.com
    You will need a Google account (free).
  2. Click "+ New notebook".
  3. Click "+ Add Source" and upload a PDF or text file.
    Use teaching materials or ask your facilitator for a sample document.
  4. In the chat box at the bottom, type:
    Generate a 5-point summary of this source.
  5. Read the summary. Click on the citation numbers (e.g., [1]) to see where information came from.
    This is "source-grounded" AI‚Äîit only uses your documents and provides citations.
  6. Create an audio version:
    Create a 2-minute audio overview of this source, suitable for student revision.
  7. Listen to the audio.

ü§î Reflection

NotebookLM only uses your documents. Unlike ChatGPT or Copilot, it will not mix in internet information or "invent" facts.

Why important? This makes NotebookLM safer for academic work. Students can summarise your materials without risk of false information.

‚úì Success Criteria

  • Uploaded a document to NotebookLM
  • Generated a summary with citations
  • Created and listened to an audio overview
  • Understand why source-grounded AI is safer
TASK 3

Using the Academic Feedback Assistant

‚è±Ô∏è 20-25 minutes

Objective: Use a custom agent to format marking feedback.

Tool: Academic Feedback Assistant

Instructions:

  1. Return to Copilot.
  2. Select Academic Feedback Assistant from the agent menu.
  3. This agent requires five pieces of information. Copy and paste this sample: 
    Final Mark: 55%
Assignment Type: 2000-word critical essay
Raw Marker Notes: student understands the topic ok. but the argument is weak. needed to use more of the core reading list texts. intro and conclusion are fine but the middle section is all over the place. referencing is not in the right style.
Marker Email: j.smith@newman.ac.uk
Marker Office Location: ED-204
  4. Paste into the chat and send.
  5. Observe the formatted output.

ü§î Reflection

The agent transformed messy notes into structured, professional feedback with institutional links.

This saves time on formatting whilst you focus on academic judgements.

Important: The agent does not "know" your discipline. It only formats what you tell it. Academic judgement remains yours.

In your own marking, replace sample notes with your actual feedback and use your email/office.

‚úì Success Criteria

  • Successfully used the Academic Feedback Assistant
  • Understand what information the agent requires
  • See how this tool could save time
  • Understand the tool formats but does not replace judgements
TASK 4

Final Reflection: Student Support

‚è±Ô∏è 15-20 minutes

Objective: Think about supporting students with AI.

Tool: Pen and paper or Word document

Write answers to these questions for group discussion:

Question 1: Productive Use

What is one way students could productively use NotebookLM or Copilot in your discipline?

Think about: summarising readings, generating revision questions, understanding complex concepts, preparing for seminars.

Question 2: Responsible Use

What is one warning you would give to ensure students use AI responsibly?

Consider: academic integrity, over-reliance, verification, understanding vs. copying, assessment regulations.

Question 3: Barriers (Bonus)

What is one barrier to implementing these tools, and how might you overcome it?

‚úì Success Criteria

  • Written responses to at least two questions
  • Responses are discipline-specific
  • Ready to share in group discussion
