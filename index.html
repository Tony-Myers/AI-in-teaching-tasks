Applied AI in Higher Education: A Practical Workshop

Welcome

Welcome to this hands-on workshop on using Applied AI to support your teaching and learning. The tools available to us are evolving rapidly, and our goal today is to provide a structured, supportive environment for you to gain practical skills and critically evaluate these technologies from the perspective of your specific disciplines.

Session Goal: A Core Set of Skills for All

Our objective is not to make everyone an AI expert, but to ensure all staff leave with a common baseline of skills. By the end of this session, you will have had hands-on experience with:

  1. Image Generation & Bias: Using Microsoft Copilot to generate images and, crucially, evaluating them for the professional and ethical biases relevant to your field.
  2. Source-Grounded AI: Using NotebookLM to summarise academic texts and create audio resources, understanding why it is safer than general AI for academic work.
  3. Custom AI Agents: Using the specialist Copilot agents we have designed, such as the Academic Feedback Assistant, to support your specific academic tasks.
  4. Student Support: Reflecting on how to guide students towards responsible and effective use of these tools.

A Pathway for Everyone

We recognise that colleagues are joining with diverse levels of experience. To make this session effective, we will not be using a one-size-fits-all approach.

  1. You Choose: You will be able to identify your current comfort level with generative AI (Novice, Beginner, Intermediate, or Advanced).
  2. Differentiated Pathways: Based on what you are comfortable with, you will follow a particular pathway, but can change during the session if you feel that would be beneficial:
    • Novices will use a detailed, step-by-step guided worksheet.
    • Beginners will use a "problem and hint" method to support them.
    • Intermediate staff will complete core tasks efficiently with a focus on critical evaluation.
    • Advanced staff can optionally review core tasks, but will primarily engage with high-level challenges requiring disciplinary expertise to critique, design, and stress-test the tools.

This model ensures that everyone is appropriately challenged and supported. Please ask questions at any time; this session is for experimentation and learning.
